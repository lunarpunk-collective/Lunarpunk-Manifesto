We are the Lunarpunks...

The Witches, Pagans & Blasphemers have come back to create a new world! We are the intergalactic and inter-dimensional movement that seeks to create a more just and equatable world by direct influence of the moon. We emulate Her natural balance and harmony, represented by the cycles of light and dark in our own lives and communities. We are Shadow Workers, creating Protopia without compromise.

We reject the dominant culture's obsession with constant growth and consumption, which has led to ecological devastation and social inequality. Contrarily, we advocate for a regenerative and restorative approach to life and community. One which recognizes the interconnectedness of all beings and all systems. Light & Dark.

We embrace technology and innovation as tools for positive change. Like any tool, we question them. All tools need be created and directed for harmony with the natural world, and serve the common good. We discourage the use of technology for profit and domination, which only perpetuates systems of oppression.

We believe in the power of community and collective action to create change. We seek to build networks of solidarity and mutual aid to resist the forces of capitalism & colonialism. Creating new modes of living that are based on our 15 principles, in no particular order. These can be summed up as the new "R&R"; Resurrection and Reciprocity. The old "R&R"; Rest and Relaxation, is but a reward or even luxury earned after Resurrection and Reciprocity is achieved. This is the paradigm we follow into the future.

We recognize that the journey towards a more just and sustainable world is long and fraught with challenges. Regardless we are committed to this path, and acknowledge that once unified we will create a better future for ourselves and all life here and beyond.

Lunarpunk 15

1.) Lunar Wisdom: We look to the cycles of the moon for guidance and inspiration, recognizing the phases which offer a natural model for balance and harmony to lead our lives and communities with. It is Her, Gaia, and similarly harmonious Deities we give thanks to.

2.) Regeneration: Sustainability isn't enough. We seek to restore and regenerate the natural systems that sustain life on this planet. We recognize that we are all interconnected and interdependent, and to think or act otherwise is harmful thinking and being. Permaculture is a vital guiding practice we seek to include in all things we do.

3.) Community: We recognize the power of collective action to create change, and we seek to build networks of solidarity and mutual aid that supports each other's well-being. Not for profit, but for purpose and survival. Community service shouldn't be a job, nor should leadership come with special rights or benefits. Service over self.

4.) Intentional Technology: We believe that technology can be a powerful tool for positive change, but only when it is sculpted intentionally with harmony and the natural world in mind. We must use sensible technological solutions, regardless of what the dominant economic paradigm believes is "better".

5.) Decentralization: We reject centralized power structures and advocate for decentralized decision-making and governance, recognizing that diversity and local control are essential for resilience and adaptability. Let those most effected make decisions that govern their own community. Question empires.

6.) Post-Capitalism: We reject the capitalistic system and it's obsession with profit and growth. While also recognizing that Capitalism is perpetuating inequality and ecological destruction. We create and leverage alternative systems wherever practicable and possible.

7.) Radical Inclusivity: We strive to create a world that is inclusive and welcoming to all, regardless of race, gender, sexuality, ability, occupation, lifestyle or any other social identity that harms none. We even strongly encourage minorities and those marginalized to lead this movement should that be their self-governed calling.

8.) Imagination: We celebrate creativity and self-expression as essential components of a vibrant and resilient culture. We actively seek to give priority to new ways of thinking and being wherever practicable and possible.

9.) Self-Sufficiency: We cultivate skills and knowledge that enables us to be more self-sufficient and less reliant on the systems of domination and control. We seek interdependence and harmony. With individual adaptability as a model for thriving communities of purpose.

10.) Revelry: We recognize that joy and celebration are essential for maintaining our spirits and sustaining our activism over the long-term. We seek to infuse our movement with these qualities, while not becoming too pacifistic, hedonistic or destructive.

11.) Revolution not Reform: We believe in the necessity of a revolutionary transformation of society, rather than incremental reforms that preserve the status quo. Baby steps are for babies, and time for reforms, performative activism, and virtue signaling is over. This is self-defense as a matter of survival.

12.) Resist Role-playing: We are wearing ancestral garb, not a fashion trend. We pay tribute to the memory of over 60,000 feminine beings burned as witches, as well as countless pagans and outcasts who were oppressed, killed, and are currently being oppressed within indigenous communities around the world. It is in their honor we bring about a better world. Some may call us Gravers or Cybergoths, but ignorant people often miss the historical context to our manner of being. We honor our ancestral roots and respective Deities, and we express this through our clothing and adornments. Drawing inspiration from our traditional and/or indigenous cultures with a future-forward twist.

13.) Defense without Compromise: There will be no compromise in defense of animals & Gaia-Mother Earth. We recognize the inherent value and sacredness of all life, and we reject the exploitation and commodification of animals and the natural world. We will not compromise in our defense of their well-being and rights to exist in peace. Apologists will be questioned and encouraged to be morally consistent. We are bringing about the 5th Wave Feminist Movement, and it is intersectional. We protect all bodies, not just ones that look and act like our own. We call to end speciesism.

14.) Security Culture: We recognize the need for security culture to protect ourselves, our communities, and our movement from state repression, surveillance, and harm from other malicious and misguided individuals. We prioritize privacy, confidentiality, and discretion in our communications with our underground actions. We are not always in our ancestral garb, and we very well could be your neighbors, your doctors, your teachers, or your favorite author. Our magick is subversive, and could be right under your nose without you realizing it.

15.) Consent Culture: Our culture is one that values and respects bodily autonomy and personal boundaries. Rejecting coercion, manipulation, and violence in it's many forms. A consent culture is crucial for creating a world free from oppression and violence. Consensual interactions are possible for many, but for some adults, and especially children, they cannot consent in the same way. We prioritize protecting those who cannot consent, and education and respect for all individuals without compromise. Purity is for drinking water, not people.

These principles are not exhaustive, and there may be other values and beliefs that are important to Lunarpunks. However these "Lunarpunk 15" are not finite. They are a living list of tenets that can serve as a foundation for understanding the core beliefs of our movement.

Microcommunities under the Lunarpunk and Solarpunk subculture are encouraged to repost this Manifesto to demonstrate their resonance with it.
